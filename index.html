Hello! My name is Bryan Espinoza-Prieto and I am from Lima, Peru. I completed my undergraduate studies at the Universidad Nacional Mayor de San Marcos, focusing on Frullania taxonomy and the bryophyte flora of Peru. My current research focuses on species complexes, evolutionary history, and distribution of this genus in the Neotropics. I combine fieldwork, herbarium studies, and genomic approaches to delimit species, infer phylogenies, and explore evolutionary patterns in the region. In my free time, I enjoy being outdoors, exploring natural areas, collecting plants, and taking photographs. I have recently started photographing bryophytes to upload to iNaturalist. When not in the field, I enjoy exploring nightlife, trying different beers, and solving puzzles. Welcome to my page!
