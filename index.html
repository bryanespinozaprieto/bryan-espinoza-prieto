Hello! My name is Bryan Espinoza-Prieto and I am from Lima, Peru. I completed my undergraduate studies at the Universidad Nacional Mayor de San Marcos, and my research has focused on the taxonomy of Frullania and the floristics of bryophytes in Peru. Currently, my work focuses on resolving species complexes, evolutionary history, and distribution of this genus in the Neotropics. I combine fieldwork, herbarium studies, and genomic approaches to delimit species, infer phylogenies, and study evolutionary patterns in the region. In my free time, I love the outdoors, exploring natural areas, collecting plants, and taking photos. I am starting to photograph bryophytes for iNaturalist. When I’m not in the field, I enjoy exploring city nightlife, trying different beers, and solving puzzles. Welcome to my website!
