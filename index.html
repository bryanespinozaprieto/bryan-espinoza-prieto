Hello! My name is Bryan, and I am from Lima, Peru. I finished my undergraduate studies in Universidad Nacional Mayor de San Marcos, and all of this time my research has focused on the taxonomy of Frullania and bryophyte floristics in Peru. Currently, my work focuses on resolving species complexes, evolutionary history, and distribution of this genus in the Neotropics. I am thinking to combine fieldwork, herbarium studies, and genomic approaches to delimit species, infer phylogenies, and study evolutionary patterns in the region. In my free time, I love to be outdoors, exploring nature areas, collecting plants and taking photos. Actually, I am starting taking photos of bryophytes to upload them in iNaturalist. If not in the field, I like to explore nightlife in cities, trying different types of beers and solving puzzles. Welcome to my page!
