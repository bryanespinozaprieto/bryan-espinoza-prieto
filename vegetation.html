Vegetation Types of Peru

Peru is one of the most biodiverse countries in the world due to its unique geography, including the Andes, the Amazon, and the Pacific coast. This incredible variety of climates and altitudes has created a wide range of ecosystems, each home to a rich diversity of plant and animal species. From lush Amazonian forests and high-altitude Andean grasslands to foggy coastal hills, Peru's landscapes host diverse vegetation types, each with flora adapted to local conditions.

The following photos show the diversity of vegetation types in the country, highlighting areas where bryophytes have been observed or reported.

Western Andean montane forests. These forests are restricted to northern Peru, reaching Ecuador and Colombia. One of the type specimens of Frullania heinrichsii Gradst., Espinoza-Prieto & J.J. Atwood was found and described in this forest type.

Transition zone between eastern montane forests and high-altitude grasslands, approximately 3300 m. Photo: Diego Paredes-Burneo.

Queñual forests. These forests, dominated by "queñua" species (Polylepis spp.), appear as patches or relics in high Andean zones, between 3500 and 4900 m.
