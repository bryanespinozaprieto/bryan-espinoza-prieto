Vegetation Types of Peru

Peru is one of the most biodiverse countries in the world, thanks to its unique geography including the Andes, Amazon, and Pacific coast. This incredible range of climates and altitudes has created many ecosystems, each home to rich diversity of plants and animals. From lush Amazonian forests and high-altitude Andean grasslands to coastal hills with fog, Peru’s landscapes host a wide variety of vegetation types, each with flora adapted to local conditions.

The following images highlight areas where bryophytes have been observed or reported.

Western montane forests, limited to northern Peru up to Ecuador and Colombia. One of the type specimens of Frullania heinrichsii Gradst., Espinoza-Prieto & J.J. Atwood was collected here.

Transition zone between eastern montane forests and high-altitude grasslands, around 3300 m. Photo: Diego Paredes-Burneo.

Queñua forests dominated by Polylepis spp., appearing as patches or relics in high-Andean zones (3500–4900 m).
