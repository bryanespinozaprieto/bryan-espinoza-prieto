Vegetation Types of Peru

Peru is one of the most biodiverse countries in the world, thanks to its unique geography that includes the Andes Mountains, the Amazon rainforest, and the Pacific coast. This incredible variety of climates and altitudes has created a wide range of ecosystems, each home to a rich array of plant and animal species. From the lush Amazonian forests and high-altitude Andean grasslands to the fog-fed coastal hills, Peru’s landscapes support diverse vegetation types, each with its own unique flora adapted to local conditions.

The following photos showcase the diversity of vegetation types across the country, highlighting areas where bryophytes have been observed or reported.

Montane forests of the western slope. These forests are restricted to the northern part of Peru, extending into Ecuador and Colombia. In this type of forest, one of the type specimens of Frullania heinrichsii Gradst., Espinoza-Prieto & J.J. Atwood was found and described.

Border zone between montane forests of the eastern slope and high-Andean grasslands, approximately 3300 m. Photo: Diego Paredes-Burneo.

Polylepis forests. These forests, dominated by species of “queñua” (Polylepis spp.), occur as patches or relicts in high-Andean areas, between 3500 and 4900 m.
